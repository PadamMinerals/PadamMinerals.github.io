Process

Processing

 Crushing & Grinding: Raw materials are crushed and ground into a fine powder, reducing them to the desired particle size for their intended use. Mixing and Blending: Depending on the product, various ingredients may be mixed and blended to achieve the desired composition and properties. Dying: If needed, the powder is dried to reduce its moisture content. This is crucial to prevent clumping and improve its flow characteristics.

Quality Control

 Ongoing Checks: Continuous monitoring is essential throughout the production cycle to ensure that quality and purity are maintained. Testing: Samples of the powdered product undergo various tests, including particle size analysis, chemical composition analysis, and other quality parameters. Regulatory Compliance: Adherence to safety standards and industry regulations is crucial to ensure the product's safety and quality.

Packaging

 Proper Packaging: Powdered products are packaged in suitable bags, depending on their specific characteristics and intended use. Protection from External Factors:: Packaging materials are chosen to protect the product from external factors such as light, humidity, and temperature fluctuations, which can degrade the quality. Labeling: Each package includes essential information such as batch numbers, manufacturing dates, and other product-specific details.

Distribution

 Handling and Transportation: The product must be handled and efficiently transported with care to prevent any damage or contamination. Shipping: The powdered product is shipped to distributors, manufacturers, or end consumers, based on its intended application and market. Storage: Distributors and manufacturers often store the powdered product in appropriate conditions to maintain its quality before use in various industries.
